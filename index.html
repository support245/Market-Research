The Challenge

The client’s executive leadership recognized the healthcare sector as a strategic growth opportunity but lacked clarity on critical market dynamics. They needed a fast, accurate path to identify where to play and how to win.

◎ Market segmentation
Which healthcare valve segments offer the most attractive market opportunities?
◎ Customer pain points
What are the key pain points driving customer purchasing decisions beyond technical specs?
◎ Differentiation
How can the client differentiate itself in a market dominated by established competitors?
◎ Go-to-market
What is the optimal strategy for a new entrant to gain traction quickly?
Our Approach

A structured 12-week engagement, with Phase 1 focused on market sizing and prioritization, and Phase 2 focused on deep customer insight.

Phase 1
Quantitative Market Analysis
  • Market attractiveness index (TAM, growth rate, margin potential)
  • Competitive intensity index (landscape and barriers)
  • Commercialization complexity (regulatory and entry friction)
Phase 2
Qualitative Customer Research
  • Interviews with key personas across the ecosystem
  • Decision process mapping and hidden buying triggers
  • Customer pain points and service expectations

Strategic Value Delivered

Validating assumptions and mitigating risk

Our research revealed critical disconnects between initial assumptions and market reality. By identifying these gaps early, we prevented the misallocation of resources and focused efforts where buyers actually value outcomes.

Assumption
Technical specs alone drive vendor selection.
Reality
Service agility and partnership models drive the final decision.

Framework development

We built a robust Composite Market Viability Scorecard to prioritize segments based on data rather than intuition.

✓ Data-driven prioritization
✓ Service-first differentiation
✓ Clear Tier 1 targets
Market Opportunity Assessment

Based on the analysis, we ranked segments using the Composite Viability Scorecard and identified primary and secondary targets.

Dialysis centers
Primary target
High viability driven by demand, switching friction, and light competition.
Clinical diagnostics
Primary target
Strong growth and recurring volume with manageable complexity.
Surgical robotics
Secondary target
High growth but higher commercialization complexity and longer cycles.
Strategic Recommendations
1

Partnership-first approach

Position as a trusted partner that treats small accounts with the same respect as large accounts, prioritizing responsiveness.

2

Frictionless experience

Implement a digital portal for instant access to CAD files and compliance docs, and a fast-track sampling process.

3

Tiered go-to-market

Aggressively pursue Tier 1 segments while maintaining selective engagement in lower-viability sectors.
